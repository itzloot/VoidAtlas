History of Space Exploration

1940s–1950s: Dawn of the Space Age

V-2 rocket (1944, Germany) — first object to reach space • Sputnik 1 (1957, USSR) — first artificial satellite • Explorer 1 (1958, USA) — first US satellite

1960s: Race to the Moon

Yuri Gagarin (1961) — first human in space • John Glenn (1962) — first American orbital flight • Apollo 11 (1969) — first Moon landing (Armstrong & Aldrin)

1970s–1980s: Space Stations & Probes

Skylab (1973) — first US station • Salyut & Mir (USSR) — long-duration stations • Voyager 1 & 2 (1977) — interstellar probes • Space Shuttle era begins (1981)

1990s–2000s: International Cooperation

Hubble Space Telescope (1990) — revolutionized astronomy • ISS construction (1998–present) • Mars rovers (Spirit/Opportunity 2004, Curiosity 2012)

2010s–2020s: Commercial & Deep Space

SpaceX Falcon 9 reusability (2015) • JWST launch (2021) • Chandrayaan-3 (2023) — first lunar south pole landing • Artemis program • Starship development

NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)

Founded

July 29, 1958 — in response to the Soviet Sputnik launch

Primary Mission

Advance space science, Earth observation, aeronautics, and human exploration

Flagship Programs

Apollo (Moon landings) • Voyager (interstellar) • Hubble (deep space) • Mars rovers • James Webb Space Telescope • Artemis (lunar return & Mars prep)

Current Priorities (2026)

Artemis III (human Moon landing) • Mars Sample Return • Deep space infrastructure

ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)

Founded

August 15, 1969 (evolved from INCOSPAR 1962)

Primary Mission

Harness space technology for national development (communication, navigation, disaster management, agriculture)

Key Achievements

Chandrayaan-1 (2008) — discovered water on Moon • Mangalyaan (2013) — first Mars orbit on first attempt • Chandrayaan-3 (2023) — first south pole soft landing

Future Plans

Gaganyaan (human spaceflight) • Aditya-L1 (solar observatory) • Indian space station by 2035

SpaceX

Founded

May 6, 2002 by Elon Musk

Primary Mission

Reduce space transportation costs and enable human settlement on Mars

Major Milestones

First private company to send spacecraft to ISS (Dragon 2012) • Falcon 9 reusability (2015) • Starlink (global internet constellation) • Crew Dragon (NASA crewed flights)

Future Vision

Starship — fully reusable vehicle for Moon, Mars, point-to-point Earth travel

Rockets & Launch Vehicles

Saturn V (NASA, 1967–1973)

Tallest & most powerful rocket ever • Used for Apollo Moon missions • Payload to LEO: 140 t • Thrust: 34 MN

Falcon 9 (SpaceX, 2010–present)

Reusable first stage • Over 300 successful launches • Payload to LEO: 22.8 t • Cost per launch: ~$67M

LVM3 / GSLV Mk III (ISRO, 2017–present)

India's heaviest operational rocket • Payload to GTO: 4 t • Used for Chandrayaan-3 & future Gaganyaan

Starship (SpaceX, in development)

Fully reusable super-heavy • Target payload to LEO: 100–150 t • Designed for Mars colonization & lunar landings

Solar System Planets

Mercury

Smallest planet • Closest to Sun • No atmosphere • Extreme temperature swings (-173°C to 427°C)

Venus

Hottest planet (462°C) • Thick CO₂ atmosphere • Runaway greenhouse effect • Slow retrograde rotation

Earth

Only known life-bearing planet • 71% water • Protective magnetic field & ozone layer

Mars

Red planet • Evidence of ancient water • Largest volcano (Olympus Mons) • Two moons

Jupiter

Largest planet • Gas giant • Great Red Spot storm • 95+ moons

Saturn

Iconic ring system • Gas giant • Least dense planet • 146+ moons

Uranus

Ice giant • Extreme axial tilt (98°) • Faint rings • 27 known moons

Neptune

Ice giant • Fastest winds (2,100 km/h) • Dark spots • 14 known moons

Black Holes

Definition

Region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing (not even light) can escape • Boundary: event horizon

Types

Stellar-mass (from massive star collapse) • Supermassive (at galaxy centers) • Primordial (theoretical)

Key Concepts

Singularity • Accretion disk • Hawking radiation • First image (2019, M87*) by Event Horizon Telescope

Myth vs Science

MYTH

Great Wall of China visible from space with naked eye

SCIENCE

Not visible from low Earth orbit without aid

MYTH

There is zero gravity in space

SCIENCE

Microgravity — continuous freefall orbit

MYTH

The Sun is yellow

SCIENCE

Emits white light; atmosphere scatters blue wavelengths

MYTH

The Moon has a permanent dark side

SCIENCE

Far side is tidally locked but receives equal sunlight
