NASA Missions Timeline

1969 – Apollo 11: First human Moon landing by Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin
1990 – Hubble Space Telescope: Deep space imaging revolution
2012 – Curiosity Rover: Mars geological exploration
2021 – James Webb Space Telescope: Infrared early universe observation
2025+ – Artemis Program: Sustainable lunar exploration and Mars preparation

Black Holes & White Holes

Black holes are regions where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape beyond the event horizon.

White holes are theoretical opposites that would only expel matter and energy, predicted by general relativity but not observed.

Myth vs Science

Myth

Great Wall of China is visible from space with naked eye

Science

Not visible without aid from low Earth orbit

Myth

There is zero gravity in space

Science

Astronauts experience microgravity due to orbital freefall

Myth

The Sun appears yellow

Science

The Sun emits white light; atmosphere scatters blue wavelengths

Myth

The Moon has a permanent dark side

Science

The far side is tidally locked but receives equal sunlight
